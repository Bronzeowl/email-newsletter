What to do when you wish there were more hours in the day...

Hi Client,

Time and balance, the two most difficult things to have control over, yet they are both the things that we do control.
—Catherine Pulsifer

This paradox is not just fascinating—it’s actually a huge source of stress. In fact, research shows that having money goes hand-in-hand with feeling like there’s never enough time in the day. Cash rich, time poor.1

So, how do you find better. balance and buy more time back for what's important to you?

A conversation can be a good first step.

Talk to your partner and/or loved ones. Start the conversation with something like:

  • The one or two things I wish I had time for are… What things do you wish you had more time for?
  • The most important things in my life are… What are the most important things to you?
  • I spend too much time doing… What things are you spending too much time doing?
  • How can we help each other do the things we want to do?
  • How can we help our child(ren) and/or grandchild(ren) when it comes to balance and getting the most value from their time?

“Time and balance [… are] things that we do control.”
 

A simple conversation can go a long way toward finding balance and getting more time for what’s most important to you and your loved ones. And that’s more likely to happen if you keep the conversation going. So, set a regular date to check in. It’ll keep you and your loved ones accountable.

If you can improve the balance in your life, you can give yourself the priceless gift of time. And that can make your relationships stronger—and your life much richer.2

In the end, you’re 100% in control of your life’s balance and the time you have. You have the power to create the balance that’s right for you—and to spend your time wisely.

So, the bottom line?

No matter what balance looks like for you, you’re limited to the same 24 hours in a day as everyone else. If you keep the lines of communication open and regularly talk about balance, time, and your goals, you’ll be well on your way to finding a better balance and getting—or staying—on the same page with your loved ones.

What does balance mean to you? How have you tried to create better balance and get more time for the things that are truly important?
